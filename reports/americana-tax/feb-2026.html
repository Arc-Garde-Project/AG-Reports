What Worked
11 confirmed leads at $55.47 CPL. The campaign generated real business results. EngageBay CRM tracked 11 real form submissions (2 test submissions excluded). At $610 total spend, that's a solid CPL for a local tax planning service where each client is worth significantly more.
Orange County converts better. OC produced 6 leads at $51.10 CPL with a 4.23% conversion rate, vs LA's 5 leads at $60.71 CPL (2.36% conversion rate). Despite LA driving more raw traffic (212 vs 142 unique visitors), OC visitors were more qualified and converted at nearly double the rate.
Ad 6 LA was the top traffic driver. It delivered 18 LP views at $4.68 per LP view with the highest CTR of any high-spend ad (3.86%) and the lowest CPC ($1.48). This ad was 3x more cost-efficient at driving traffic than the next best performer.
Static Image 2 dominated retargeting. It captured 58.2% of all retargeting spend across 6 weeks. The algorithm consistently chose it over the other 4 images. Static 4 was a distant second at 23.2%.
What Needs Attention
Meta pixel is not tracking conversions. The pixel reported 0 leads while EngageBay CRM confirmed 11. The "Lead" event is not firing on form submission or thank-you page load. This means Meta's algorithm cannot optimize for conversions, it is optimizing blind. Fixing the pixel will let the algorithm find more people like the 11 who already converted.
Hook rate is well below benchmark. Only 6.03% of viewers watched past the first 25% of the video. The industry benchmark is 30%+. The first 3 seconds are not grabbing attention. Every video needs a stronger opening hook to stop the scroll.
LA drives traffic but OC converts better. LA's cost per LP view ($8.43) is cheaper, but OC's conversion rate (4.23%) is nearly double LA's (2.36%). The budget split should factor in both traffic cost and conversion quality, not just volume.
Recommended Next Steps
1. Fix the Meta pixel immediately. The #1 priority. 11 leads came through and Meta has no idea. The pixel needs to fire a "Lead" event on form submission or thank-you page load. Once fixed, Meta's algorithm can optimize for conversions instead of just link clicks, which should dramatically improve lead quality and lower CPL.
2. Increase budget on OC. OC converts at 4.23% vs LA's 2.36%, with a $51.10 CPL vs $60.71. Consider a 60/40 OC/LA budget split. OC visitors are more likely to fill out the form, even though LA drives more raw traffic.
3. New video creative with a stronger hook. The videos hold well once people watch (38.5% hold, 21.1% completion), but only 6% get past the first few seconds. Lead with the pain point or a bold visual in the first frame. The audience will stay if the hook lands.
4. Kill underperforming retargeting creatives. Static 1, 3, and 5 combined account for only 18.7% of retargeting spend. Replace them with 3 new test images while keeping Static 2 (top performer) and Static 4 (second place) running.
5. Scale to $35-50/day after pixel fix. The campaign proved it can generate leads at ~$55 CPL. Once the pixel is firing and Meta can optimize for conversions, increase daily budget from $20 to $35-50 on the winning combinations. Monitor CPL for the first 7 days of scaling.
Final Verdict
The campaign is working. The tracking is not.

After 6 weeks and $610.13 in spend, the campaign delivered 11 confirmed leads at $55.47 CPL, 534 ThruPlays at $1.00 each, and 354 unique landing page visitors with a 3.11% conversion rate. The algorithm found winning ads (Ad 6 LA for traffic, Ad 2 OC for conversions) and a winning retargeting creative (Static 2).

The critical issue is the broken Meta pixel. With 0 of 11 leads tracked, Meta's algorithm is running blind. It cannot optimize for the people most likely to convert. Fixing the pixel is the single highest-leverage move. Once Meta can see conversions, CPL should drop significantly as the algorithm targets people similar to the 11 who already converted.

Bottom line: Fix the pixel, lean into OC (higher conversion rate), refresh the video hooks, and scale to $35-50/day. The foundation is solid.
